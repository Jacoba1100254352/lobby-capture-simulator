\begin{abstract}
Regulatory capture persists not only because organized interests spend money, but because they can move influence across venues when reforms close visible routes. This article introduces the Lobby Capture Simulator, a Java-based agent simulation in which lobby organizations and funders allocate finite budgets across access, technical and public information, legal pressure, campaign finance, dark money, revolving-door access, intermediaries, and defensive anti-reform spending. Public administrative data are used as distributional anchors and validation diagnostics rather than causal estimates. Synthetic experiments illustrate how reforms that suppress visible channels can leave modeled hidden influence intact through venue, messenger, financing, and intermediary substitution.
\end{abstract}
