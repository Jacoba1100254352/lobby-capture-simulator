\abstract[ABSTRACT]{Regulatory capture persists not only because organized interests spend money, but because they can move influence across venues when reforms close visible routes. This article introduces the Lobby Capture Simulator, a Java-based agent simulation in which lobby organizations and funders allocate finite budgets across access, information distortion, public campaigns, litigation threats, campaign finance, dark money, revolving-door access, and defensive anti-reform spending. The current prototype uses public administrative data as distributional anchors and validation diagnostics rather than causal estimates; its first results are mechanism checks showing that anti-capture bundles can reduce observed capture while hidden influence survives through venue, messenger, and financing substitution.}
